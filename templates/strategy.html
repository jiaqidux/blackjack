Blackjack Optimal Strategy Table

These strategy tables serves as a comprehensive guide for making mathematically optimal decisions during a game of Blackjack. The decisions are calculated based on two primary variables: the total value of the player's current hand and the specific upcard shown by the dealer.

To use the tables effectively, hands are categorized into three distinct types:

  • Hard Totals: Hands that do not contain an Ace, or where the Ace must be counted as 1 to avoid busting.
  • Soft Totals: Hands containing an Ace that can still be counted as either 1 or 11 without exceeding 21.
  • Pairs: Two cards of the identical rank, opening up the strategic choice of splitting the hand into two separate bets.

The mathematical calculations used to generate these tables consider the following guidelines:

  • The simulation uses a shoe containing 6 decks of cards.
  • The dealer hits on a soft 17.
  • The player is allowed to double down after a split (DAS).
  • Only original bets are lost in the event of a dealer blackjack.
By consistently aligning your moves with this chart, you eliminate guesswork and mitigate the house edge, ensuring you play each hand with the highest statistical advantage possible.
